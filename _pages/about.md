I am a master's student at the University of Edinburgh, being supervised by <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=zLDAY8QAAAAJ&hl=en" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. Jeff Pan</a>. Previously, I was a predoctoral researcher at the Indian Institute of Science (Bengaluru), where I worked with <a href="https://www.vistalabiisc.com/P_portfolio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. Punit Rathore</a> on projects related to graph neural networks and computer vision. I also worked with <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Gu8FjdwAAAAJ&hl=en" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. Sundeep Chepuri</a> on one of these projects.

For my master's thesis, I am designing a benchmark to evaluate **multimodal, multi-turn Deep Research Agents (DRAs)** built on the LangChain Open Deep Research framework, with a focus on systematically assessing agent reasoning, tool use, and information synthesis during long research tasks. The benchmark aims to analyse multimodal grounding in DRAs by testing whether agents can accurately extract and integrate information from figures and tables encountered during a live research loop. It further investigates whether DRAs regress on previously fetched correct information when incorporating feedback-style prompts from a user simulator.

At the University of Edinburgh, I have taken relevant courses including Accelerated Natural Language Processing, Advanced Topics in Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning Systems, and Probabilistic Modelling and Reasoning. Please refer to my [resume](https://sabharwalrishabh.github.io/assets/pdf/Rishabh_Sabharwal_Resume.pdf) for further details on projects and work experience.
